WIRE TRANSFER RISK INDICATORS — INTERNAL RISK MEMO
====================================================
Classification: INTERNAL — Payment Operations & Compliance
Effective Date: March 2025
Scope: All domestic and cross-border wire transfers processed through Elysium systems

OVERVIEW
--------
Wire transfers remain the highest-risk payment channel due to speed of settlement, difficulty of reversal, and cross-jurisdictional complexity. This memo establishes the risk indicator framework for automated monitoring and manual review of wire transfer activity.

RISK INDICATOR CATEGORY 1: AMOUNT-BASED TRIGGERS
---------------------------------------------------
- Single wire ≥ $50,000 to a new beneficiary (first-time relationship)
- Aggregate outgoing wires ≥ $100,000 in a 7-day rolling window
- Wire amount exceeds 3x the customer's historical average wire amount
- Wire amount represents ≥ 60% of current account balance
- Multiple wires of $9,500–$9,999 within 5 business days (structuring indicator)

Severity Scoring:
  - 1 trigger: LOW (score 2.0) — log and monitor
  - 2 triggers: MEDIUM (score 5.0) — queue for next-day review
  - 3+ triggers: HIGH (score 8.5) — real-time alert to compliance analyst

RISK INDICATOR CATEGORY 2: BENEFICIARY-BASED TRIGGERS
-------------------------------------------------------
- Beneficiary bank located in FATF grey-list or black-list jurisdiction
- Beneficiary account opened within past 30 days
- Beneficiary name contains known shell company indicators (e.g., "Holdings Ltd", "Trading Corp", "Global Enterprises" with no web presence)
- Same beneficiary receiving wires from 3+ unrelated originators within 30 days
- Beneficiary account has received and immediately forwarded funds (pass-through behavior with <24-hour hold time)

Severity Scoring:
  - FATF grey-list destination: Automatic MEDIUM (score 5.5)
  - FATF black-list destination: Automatic HIGH (score 9.0) + analyst alert
  - Pass-through pattern detected: HIGH (score 8.0) + SAR consideration

RISK INDICATOR CATEGORY 3: BEHAVIORAL TRIGGERS
------------------------------------------------
- Wire initiated outside customer's normal business hours (based on 90-day profile)
- Wire instruction submitted via a channel the customer has never previously used
- Multiple wires to different beneficiaries within the same 60-minute session
- Customer requests expedited processing with no clear business justification
- Wire instruction contains vague purpose codes ("miscellaneous", "personal", "other")

Severity Scoring:
  - Off-hours initiation alone: LOW (score 1.5)
  - New channel + off-hours: MEDIUM (score 4.5)
  - Multiple same-session wires + vague purpose: HIGH (score 7.5)

RISK INDICATOR CATEGORY 4: CORRIDOR-SPECIFIC TRIGGERS
-------------------------------------------------------
High-risk corridors requiring Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD):
  - US → Nigeria: Threshold lowered to $3,000
  - US → UAE (free zone entities): Threshold lowered to $10,000
  - UK → Russia (via third country): Any amount triggers review
  - Singapore → Myanmar: Any amount triggers review
  - Any jurisdiction → Iran: Blocked per sanctions program

AUTOMATED RESPONSE MATRIX
--------------------------
Risk Score 0–3.0: Process normally, log for batch review
Risk Score 3.1–5.0: Process with 4-hour review window; analyst can reverse within window
Risk Score 5.1–7.5: Hold pending analyst approval (target: 2-hour SLA)
Risk Score 7.6–10.0: Block transaction; immediate escalation to BSA Officer; 24-hour SAR decision required

EXCEPTIONS
----------
- Pre-approved recurring wires (e.g., payroll, vendor payments with established pattern) may receive a -2.0 score adjustment
- VIP/institutional accounts with enhanced KYC may receive a -1.5 adjustment (requires annual recertification)
