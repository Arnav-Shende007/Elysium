FRAUD PATTERN RED FLAGS — INTERNAL RISK MEMO
=============================================
Classification: INTERNAL — Financial Crime Unit
Effective Date: February 2025
Prepared by: Transaction Surveillance Team

OVERVIEW
--------
This document catalogs the principal fraud typologies encountered across FinPulse AI's transaction monitoring ecosystem. Each pattern includes detection indicators, typical monetary thresholds, and recommended automated alert configurations.

PATTERN 1: VELOCITY STACKING
-----------------------------
Description: Rapid succession of transactions designed to move funds before detection systems aggregate activity.
Indicators:
  - More than 8 transactions within a 30-minute window from the same account
  - Individual amounts below SAR filing thresholds ($3,000–$9,999)
  - Transactions span multiple merchant categories (suggesting automated distribution)
  - Aggregate amount in 24-hour window exceeds $50,000
Historical Hit Rate: 73% of flagged velocity patterns confirmed as suspicious upon investigation
Recommended Alert Threshold: ≥6 transactions in 20 minutes with aggregate >$15,000

PATTERN 2: ROUND-DOLLAR STRUCTURING
-------------------------------------
Description: Deliberate fragmentation of large transactions into round-dollar amounts to evade Currency Transaction Report (CTR) thresholds.
Indicators:
  - Multiple transactions of exactly $9,000, $9,500, or $4,999 within 48 hours
  - Same originator, different beneficiary accounts
  - Transactions initiated from different channels (mobile, branch, ATM) to obscure pattern
  - No corresponding commercial invoices or documented purpose
Historical Hit Rate: 68% confirmation rate when combined with new-account flags
Recommended Alert Threshold: ≥3 round-dollar transactions within 72 hours from same originator

PATTERN 3: ACCOUNT TAKEOVER CHAIN
------------------------------------
Description: Compromised credentials used to initiate unauthorized transfers, typically following a phishing or SIM-swap attack.
Indicators:
  - Password reset or device change followed by wire transfer within 2 hours
  - Login from new IP geolocation inconsistent with customer profile
  - Beneficiary added and funds transferred within single session
  - Transfer amount represents >80% of available balance
  - Customer contact information changed prior to transfer
Historical Hit Rate: 91% confirmation when password reset + new beneficiary + large transfer occur within 4 hours
Recommended Alert Threshold: New device + new beneficiary + transfer >$5,000 within same session

PATTERN 4: LAYERING THROUGH CRYPTO OFF-RAMPS
----------------------------------------------
Description: Fiat-to-crypto-to-fiat conversion chain used to obscure fund origins.
Indicators:
  - Wire transfer to known crypto exchange deposit addresses
  - Followed by incoming transfer from different crypto exchange within 24–72 hours
  - Amount received is 85–95% of amount sent (accounting for exchange fees and conversion spread)
  - Pattern repeats 3+ times per month
  - Beneficiary exchanges registered in jurisdictions with weak crypto regulation
Historical Hit Rate: 62% confirmation rate; high false-positive rate from legitimate crypto traders
Recommended Alert Threshold: ≥2 round-trip crypto conversions in 30 days with aggregate >$25,000

PATTERN 5: INVOICE MANIPULATION
---------------------------------
Description: Inflated or fictitious invoices used to justify large cross-border payments.
Indicators:
  - Payment amounts that are exact multiples of previous invoices (suggesting copy-paste inflation)
  - Supplier entities incorporated within past 6 months
  - Supplier registered address matches known virtual office providers
  - Trade volume inconsistent with supplier's reported revenue or headcount
  - Goods described as "consulting services" or "technical advisory" with no deliverables documented
Historical Hit Rate: 55% confirmation; requires manual document review to confirm
Recommended Alert Threshold: Cross-border payment >$20,000 to entity <12 months old with "services" description

PATTERN 6: DORMANT ACCOUNT ACTIVATION
---------------------------------------
Description: Previously inactive accounts suddenly showing high-volume activity, indicating potential sale or compromise of the account.
Indicators:
  - No transactions for 90+ days followed by >5 transactions in 48 hours
  - Account holder demographics inconsistent with new transaction patterns (e.g., retiree account suddenly receiving international wires)
  - Contact information updated within 7 days of reactivation
Historical Hit Rate: 78% confirmation when combined with contact-change flag
Recommended Alert Threshold: Account inactive >60 days with >$10,000 aggregate activity within first 72 hours of reactivation

COMPOSITE SCORING
-----------------
When multiple patterns co-occur, apply multiplicative risk weighting:
  - 1 pattern detected: Base risk score
  - 2 patterns detected: 2.5x multiplier
  - 3+ patterns detected: 5.0x multiplier, automatic case escalation to Level 3 investigator
