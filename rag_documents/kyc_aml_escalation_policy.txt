1. PURPOSE
----------
This policy defines the escalation framework for Know Your Customer (KYC) and Anti-Money Laundering (AML) issues identified during customer onboarding, periodic reviews, and transaction monitoring. It ensures consistent, timely, and well-documented escalation of potential compliance risks across all business lines.

2. ESCALATION TIERS
---------------------

TIER 1: STANDARD ESCALATION (Response: 5 business days)
  Triggers:
  - Incomplete or inconsistent customer identification documents
  - Minor discrepancies in beneficial ownership declarations (e.g., percentage differences < 5%)
  - Customer's industry classification changes to a higher-risk category
  - Negative news screening returns results with low relevance score (< 60%)
  
  Actions:
  - Relationship Manager sends KYC remediation request to customer
  - 30-day cure period for document submission
  - If unresolved after 30 days, auto-escalate to Tier 2
  
  Decision Authority: Level 1 KYC Analyst

TIER 2: ENHANCED ESCALATION (Response: 48 hours)
  Triggers:
  - Customer unable or unwilling to provide source of funds documentation
  - Beneficial ownership cannot be verified through independent sources
  - Negative news screening returns results with moderate relevance (60–80%)
  - Customer's transaction activity materially deviates from stated business purpose
  - PEP (Politically Exposed Person) status identified or changed
  - Customer operating in FATF grey-list jurisdiction
  
  Actions:
  - Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) review initiated
  - Senior analyst reviews full customer file, transaction history (12 months), and all prior alerts
  - EDD report prepared with risk assessment and recommendation
  - Customer relationship placed on "enhanced monitoring" pending resolution
  
  Decision Authority: Level 2 KYC Analyst + Compliance Manager sign-off

TIER 3: CRITICAL ESCALATION (Response: 24 hours)
  Triggers:
  - Sanctions match confirmed (OFAC, EU, UN)
  - Customer identified as agent of a sanctioned entity or government
  - Negative news screening returns high-relevance results (> 80%) involving financial crime, terrorism, or trafficking
  - Law enforcement inquiry or subpoena received regarding customer
  - Customer attempts to close account and withdraw all funds immediately after receiving KYC request
  - Internal fraud or collusion suspected involving bank employee
  
  Actions:
  - Immediate transaction freeze on all customer accounts
  - BSA Officer notified within 2 hours
  - Legal counsel engaged for sanctions matches or law enforcement matters
  - SAR filing decision within 24 hours
  - If sanctions confirmed: Account blocked; OFAC reported within 10 business days
  
  Decision Authority: BSA Officer + Legal Counsel + Chief Compliance Officer

TIER 4: EXECUTIVE ESCALATION (Response: Same day)
  Triggers:
  - Systemic compliance failure identified (e.g., monitoring system gap affecting multiple customers)
  - Regulatory examination finding requiring immediate remediation
  - Media exposure risk related to customer relationship
  - Correspondent banking relationship flagged by upstream/downstream bank
  
  Actions:
  - Executive Compliance Committee convened (CCO, General Counsel, CRO, CEO)
  - External counsel engaged if regulatory action anticipated
  - Board Risk Committee notified if potential financial impact exceeds $5 million
  - Regulatory self-disclosure evaluated
  
  Decision Authority: Executive Compliance Committee

3. PERIODIC REVIEW SCHEDULE
-----------------------------
Customer Risk Rating    | Review Frequency | EDD Required
High Risk               | Annual           | Yes
Medium Risk             | Biennial         | Trigger-based
Low Risk                | Triennial        | No
PEP (any risk level)    | Annual           | Yes
Correspondent Banks     | Annual           | Yes

4. DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS
-------------------------------
All escalations must include:
  - Customer identification details and risk rating
  - Description of triggering event with supporting evidence
  - Timeline of actions taken
  - Analyst assessment and recommendation
  - Decision rationale (approve, restrict, exit, report)
  - Supervisor sign-off with date

Documentation retention: Minimum 7 years from account closure or escalation resolution, whichever is later.

5. CUSTOMER EXIT PROCEDURES
-----------------------------
When a decision is made to exit a customer relationship:
  - 30-day notice provided (unless immediate exit required for sanctions/law enforcement)
  - All pending transactions reviewed and processed or returned
  - Account closure documented with full rationale
  - Customer added to internal "Do Not Onboard" list
  - SAR filed if suspicious activity was the basis for exit
  - No tipping off: Customer must NOT be informed that a SAR has been filed

6. TRAINING
-----------
  - All customer-facing staff: Annual KYC/AML awareness training (2 hours)
  - KYC analysts: Quarterly specialized training on emerging typologies (4 hours)
  - Compliance officers: Annual regulatory update seminar + external conference attendance
  - Training completion tracked; non-completion escalated to line manager after 14 days
